Laurel Run
Park

Laurel Run Park is a special treasure trove of wildlife, flora, and activities. From beautiful nature walks and incredible fishing, to an exciting disc golf course and brand new playground, this incredible park is perfect for you and your family's weekend trips. Special events are hosted within the park throughout the year. Check our Facebook for schedule updates. Laurel Run Park is open all year, excluding Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day, with hours varying depending on the season. Shelter reservations are available for a fee.

The hiking trails are incredible. There are 37.5 miles of trails in conjunction with Bays Mountain Park, and they feature such things as: an overlook that rises 300â€™ above the park to give you a unique perspective of the surrounding area, old homesteads, an unfinished cabin and smoke house, and two waterfalls. Laurel Falls is a scenic, 20-minute hike up the trail. An additional 20-minute hike will take you to Kiner Creek Falls. Plan on spending the day exploring all we have to offer.

The hiking

Laurel Run Park is home to an incredibly popular 9-hole disc golf course, which has hosted tournaments and brought a lot of excitement to the area. Laurel Run also sports a beautifully renovated playground for children, basketball and tennis courts, a baseball field, and a walking trail by the river. Laurel Run's dog-friendly, so make sure to bring the whole family.

The Holston River, the powerful backdrop for the Mel Gibson and Sissy Spacek film, The River, stretches along Laurel Run Park. It is home to diverse wildlife, large catfish, and exciting fun boating along its waters.
